HOW TO MAKE NIGERIAN JOLLOF RICE

Jollof rice is a typical west African dish served daily in homes.
It is also used during celebrations such as birthdays, baby naming ceremonies,
weddings and festive periods like Christmas.

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time: 10 mins
  • Cook Time: 45 mins
  • Total Time: 1 hour
  • Servings: 4
  • Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients

For the base:
  • red bell pepper: 1 (chopped)
  • tomatoes: 2 medium (chopped)
  • onion: 1 red onion (chopped)
  • red scotch bonnet pepper: 2 (chopped)
  • garlic: 3 cloves (minced)
  • ginger: 25g (grated)
  • vegetable oil: 150ml
For the rice:
  • long grain parboiled rice: 2 cups (washed)
  • chicken stock: 2 1/2 cups
  • tomato paste: 2 tablespoons
  • bay leaves: 2
  • thyme: 1 teaspoon (dried)
  • curry powder: 1 teaspoon
  • salt: to taste
  • black pepper: to taste

Instructions

  1. Place the base ingredients in a blender and blitz until smooth.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large Dutch oven set over a medium heat. Add the onion
    and cook, stirring occasionally, for 3 minutes, then add the tomato purée and cook,
    stirring frequently, until it begins to darken, 3 to 5 minutes,
  3. Pour in the blended base, stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to
    medium-low and partially cover the pot with the lid – it will splatter! Cook, stirring occasionally,
    until the sauce is reduced by about a third of its original volume and the
    oil begins to separate from the sauce, 12 to 15 minutes.
  4. Stir in the curry powder, thyme, stock cubes, bay leaves and water.
    Season generously with salt and pepper, to taste, then cover and bring to a boil over medium-
    high heat.
  5. Add the rice, stir to combine, then cover and reduce the heat to low. Cook for 25-30 minutes,
    or until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork before serving.
  6. Serve with fried plantains, steamed vegetables, or your choice of protein.

Tips

  • Jollof rice is a little tricky to master. After the 25 minutes of cooking time, if the rice is sauce-logged, take the lid off
    the pot to cook off any excess moisture. If the rice is dry and/or undercooked, stir in 100 ml to 200 ml water, cover
    and continue to steam over low heat.
